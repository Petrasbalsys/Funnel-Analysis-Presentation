Key Findings & Actionable Insights

Key Findings

Friction Points

The most significant user drop-offs occur early in the journey and critically during checkout at the 'Add Payment Info' step.

Promotion Impact

While promotions drive initial interest (view_promotion), they currently show low conversion to purchase.

User Behavior

Highly engaged users and returning users demonstrate higher conversion rates. The path to purchase often involves multiple sessions.

Segmentation

Funnel shapes and drop-off points remain largely consistent across different splits, suggesting core usability issues rather than segment-specific problems.

Data Context

Pseudo_ID tracking limits true cross-device user identification. Focusing only on the Top 3 countries excludes a substantial portion (42%) of user sessions.

Actionable Insights

Optimize Early Engagement

Focus on improving landing pages, product discovery, and the transition from view_item to add_to_cart to combat the initial drop-off.

Streamline Checkout

Prioritize investigating and simplifying the 'Add Payment Info' stage. Consider usability testing, reviewing payment options, and ensuring clarity around costs.

Re-evaluate Promotions

Analyze the ROI of current promotions. Test different offers or placements to improve their effectiveness in driving purchases, not just views.

Nurture High-Value Users

Develop strategies to encourage user engagement and retain returning visitors, as they are more likely to convert.

Investigate US Traffic

Analyze traffic sources for the US segment to understand the high volume and identify potential non-genuine traffic.
